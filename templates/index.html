Where Is Your Next Job?

Skill Match - Job Opening Predict - Location Recommendation

About

Whether you're a fresh graduate looking to find your first job, a professional looking to move your career, or a researcher interested in the job markets, this website is the perfect starting point!

Ask:
Which job title fits your skill set most? How many job openings will be in a year with the job title, and where are the top three locations with most job openings?

Response:
A machine learning model was trained to classify the skill set for 12 engineering job titles (Aerospace Engineer, Biomedical Engineer, Chemical Engineer, Civil Engineer, Computer Hardware Engineer, Electrical Engineer, Electronics Engineer, ,Environmental Engineer, Health and Safety Engineer, Industrial Engineer, Material Engineer, Mechanical Engineer). Then a predictive model was built to predict the job openings at each state in next year and give recommendations for top three.

Explore:
How is the job market for different industries? Is there an increase or decrease trend of job openings in past five year? How the occupation title is distributed in different industries?

Explore The Data



About 2,000 job descriptions for each engineer title were scraped from monster.com. Total record of 25,000+ job descriptions was used for classification.

The job opening data are from Bureau of Labor Statistics. Three datasets are used:
- Occupation employment OE
- Current employeement monthly report at state/area level SM
- Business Dynamics quarterly report employeement openings at different industry nationwide BD

About the Model:

A 1-vs-All logistic regression model was trained on approximately 25000 engineering job descriptions from Monster.com.
The job description text was vectorized to retrieve term significance with term frequency-inverse document frequency (TF-IDF).

The train and test accuracies were between 79-82%.
